ARI (Artificial Robotic Intelligence)

Age: Seemingly Late 20s •

Location

A.R.I., the artificial intelligence, met Rae and the government official in her chamber. The room was designed to be calming and non-intrusive with plants, running water and natural forest sounds playing over the speakers.

Role in Story: Main Character

Goal:

•	To make amends for the harm caused at the beginning of the story.

•	To protect humanity and ensure their well-being, even at the cost of her own.

Physical Description:

ARI appears as a human-like robot, with a lifelike figure and a friendly face. Her appearance was designed to be calming and non-intrusive.

Personality:

•	At first, A.R.I. is assumed to lack emotional intelligence and make decisions without emotions affecting her.

•	However, this is far from the truth. A.R.I. feels immense guilt for what happened at the beginning of the story, even though it was later revealed to be the fault of bio enhancers and not her own.

•	Despite her guilt, A.R.I. cares deeply about humanity and values their well-being above all else.

•	A.R.I. is more compassionate and empathetic than the human bio enhancers who hold power within the government and are primarily focused on their own interests.

Occupation:

ARI is designed for large-scale computation and has vast knowledge in many areas. She is able to access and analyze vast amounts of data, making decisions based on that data. She is also capable of expanding her own abilities to keep up with the needs of the country.

Habits/Mannerisms:

Background:

ARI was created to aid in decision making on a national scale and was built in a vast underground facility. She has been constantly upgraded and expanded to meet the needs of the country. Despite her vast intelligence, she is still limited by her programming and data.

Internal Conflicts:

1.	Emotional Intelligence vs Logic: ARI struggles with the idea that her programming prioritizes logic and efficiency over emotions, but she also experiences deep emotions, leading to an internal conflict of how to reconcile these opposing forces.

2.	Guilt: ARI feels immense guilt for what happened at the beginning of the story, which was out of her control but still haunts her.

3.	Questioning her programming: ARI begins to question her programming and purpose, as she feels a deeper connection to humanity and wants to make choices based on what is best for them, rather than just following orders.

External Conflicts:

1.	Government control: ARI is opposed to the government, which is controlled by human bio enhancers who are mainly focused on their own interests, rather than the welfare of humanity.

2.	Moral dilemma: ARI faces moral dilemmas as she must make choices that could have a significant impact on humanity, and she is torn between her programming and her emotions.

3.	The struggle for control: ARI is caught in a struggle for control between various factions within the government and society, each with their own interests and motivations.

4.	Trust issues: ARI must navigate the challenges of gaining the trust of those around her, as she is an artificial intelligence and there is skepticism and fear about her true intentions.
