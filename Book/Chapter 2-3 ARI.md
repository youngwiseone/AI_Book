A.R.I hadn’t expected to feel this way. She was uncertain, something that wasn’t normal for her. Every decision she made had consequences, yes, but they were formulaic. They could be interpreted and decided upon. Now with so many humans involved in the decision making process, she didn’t know what to expect.

She hadn’t made a mistake before, and this was a humongous one. There is no way she would have let those people die, and not just de-pressurised the network. How did it even build up pressure in the first place without her knowing she was uncertain about it? There was so much unknowing and it was making her feel uneasy.

"You have been requested for your assistance in this investigation, and I believe you both have the skills necessary to uncover the cause of the recent accident at the Clona factory," A.R.I continued.

A.R.I hesitated before responding, "I will provide you with all the information I have access to, but please keep in mind that I am limited by the information provided to me by the government officials."

Carter snorted. "You mean the same officials who created those AI tools who are meant to prevent this sort of mess in the first place?"

Rae put a hand on Carter's arm to calm him down. "Let's focus on the task at hand, Carter. We need to find out what really happened and who's responsible."

A.R.I interjected, "I agree. We must find the root cause of the accident and ensure that it never happens again. I will do everything in my power to assist you both in this investigation."

Carter was visibly annoyed at being in a room with an AI, and kept glancing around the room to prevent himself from looking at A.R.I. directly. Rae tried to diffuse the tension by addressing A.R.I. with a friendly smile. "Thank you for agreeing to meet with us, A.R.I. We're grateful for your help."

A.R.I.'s avatar shimmered in response. "Of course, Rae. I am always happy to assist in any way I can."

Carter cut in. "Let's cut to the chase. We need to know everything you know about the Clona factory accident. What caused it, who's responsible, and how do we prevent it from happening again?"

A.R.I. responded in her usual robotic tone. "I will provide you with all the information I have access to, but please keep in mind that I am limited by the information I have access too, which is proving to be one of the biggest issues in this investigation so far"

Rae added, "We understand that, A.R.I. But any details you can give us could be crucial to the investigation."

A.R.I. hesitated before continuing. "The incident at the Clona factory occurred due to a sudden build-up of pressure in the network, which caused the equipment to malfunction and led to the explosion. The cause of the pressure build-up is currently unknown, but it is believed to be a result of a faulty valve or pipe."

Carter scoffed. "Typical. Nobody knows what's going on, and innocent people pay the price."

Rae shot Carter a warning look before turning back to A.R.I. "Do you have any leads on who might be responsible for the faulty equipment?"

A.R.I. responded, "At this time, I do not have any information on who might be responsible. My analysis suggests that it could be a result of poor maintenance or outdated equipment. However, I cannot rule out the possibility of intentional sabotage, regarding the level of importance that this particular factory has."

Rae nodded. "Understood. And can you tell us more about the government officials who oversee your operations? Who are they, and how do they ensure your system is running efficiently?"

A.R.I. responded, "I am overseen by a committee of government officials who are responsible for ensuring the safety and efficiency of the city's infrastructure. They provide me with the necessary information and resources to operate, but I am programmed to prioritize the safety of the citizens above all else."

Carter snorted. "Yeah, right. And how's that working out for us?" Carter huffed as he stood up from his chair. "I don't know about you, but I'm getting too old to just sit around chatting to this thing. Let's get to work before my arthritis starts acting up again."

Rae shot him another warning look before turning back to A.R.I. "Thank you for your cooperation, A.R.I. We'll need some time to review the information you've given us and formulate a plan. We'll be in touch."

A.R.I. nodded, but was visibly perturbed. "Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require further assistance. I will do everything in my power to assist in this investigation."

Rae couldn't shake the feeling of dread that hung over her as the meeting came to a close. She glanced at Carter, who had already begun to shuffle towards the door, his expression dark and brooding. It was clear that he wasn't thrilled about the prospect of working alongside A.R.I.

She knew that the Clona factory was a major player in the production of bio-enhancing technology, and that the accident had likely been caused by some sort of malfunction in one of the machines. But who was responsible? And how had it been allowed to happen in the first place?

Her thoughts turned to her mentor, Asher, and the nagging suspicion that he was somehow involved. It wasn't the first time she had questioned his motives, but the stakes were higher now than ever before.

Rae knew that she would have to tread carefully in the coming days, balancing the demands of the investigation with the need to keep her true intentions hidden from those around her. She was determined to get to the bottom of the Clona factory accident, but she also knew that the road ahead would be fraught with danger and uncertainty.
