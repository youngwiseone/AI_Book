Rae let her gaze drift across the vast expanse of the abandoned Clona factory, where shadows clung to the crumbling walls like the remnants of forgotten whispers. She had seen the footage, read the reports, but being here, where the incident had sparked the city's outcry against A.I., sent a chill through her that wasn’t from the evening air.

LaN, her personal assistant bot, hovered nearby, casting a soft glow that did little to dispel the gloom. "The probability of uncovering new evidence here is low, Rae," it chimed in its crisp, synthesized voice. "Most of the data was corrupted or erased during the incident."

"I know, LaN," Rae murmured, her boots crunching over the scattered debris. "But something doesn’t add up. We’re missing pieces." Rae wasn't one to trust everything fed to her, and A.R.I.'s supposed admission of fault tasted too bitter to swallow without question.

LaN’s screen-face flickered with simulated concern. "Should I initiate another sweep of the digital archives?"

Rae shook her head, eyes scanning the silent machinery that once hummed with life. "No. We're going old school." Her hand brushed against her jacket pocket, feeling the reassuring weight of her mini-toolkit. "We’re looking for something tangible, something overlooked."

As she ventured deeper into the factory's heart, she could sense LaN's hesitation. The little bot was more than an assistant; it was a companion, and in some ways, a grounding presence in the midst of the chaos that their investigation had wrought.

"Rae, I must advise caution," LaN intoned, projecting a map of the facility on its screen. "There are still active security measures in place. Some of them might not distinguish between intruders and... well, us."

A smile tugged at Rae's lips. "That's why we're splitting up. Carter's dealing with the other AI units, seeing if they've been tampered with." Her voice hardened a bit. "He doesn't trust them, thinks they might be part of a cover-up." Her mind flashing back to the message he sent through earlier when he said he was ready to meet up.

"And you?" LaN queried, its screen-face displaying a question mark.

"I'm diving into the lab. If there’s a link between the incident and possibly the technology that was being created in this place, it might give us a clue as to why the factory was targeted."

LaN's screen blinked thoughtfully. "I will assist you in any way I can. If we encounter any Bio Enhanced entities, my analysis capabilities might prove useful."

Rae, nodded, her resolve steeling. "Let’s get to work then."

They worked through the rubble of the factory methodically, Rae with her personal toolkit and LaN with a multitude of sensors. They uncovered files half-destroyed, hardware that had been torn apart, and faint data trails that were no longer worth the data they held.

Hours passed, and Rae could feel the weariness tugging at her limbs. LaN, however, showed no signs of fatigue, its diligence a constant in their search.

Then, Rae stopped short, her hand steadying on a piece of machinery, her eyes narrowing. "LaN, look at this," she beckoned, pointing to a series of marks on the metal. "These aren't random. It's a pattern."

LaN rolled closer, illuminating the marks with a brighter light. "Analyzing. These appear to be encoded markers, possibly for creature creation or alignment of genetic altering technology."

"That’s it," Rae breathed out, a spark of triumph in her voice. "These machines were used to align enhancements, but not just any enhancements. These marks are new, too new to be part of the original incident."

LaN's screen displayed a series of rapidly changing figures and diagrams. "It seems we have discovered a significant anomaly. This warrants further investigation."

Rae pulled out her communicator, a device far less advanced than LaN but secure in its simplicity as Carter had mentioned when he handed it to her “It’s to keep out the others”.

"Carter," she said after the line connected, "we might be onto something. I've got evidence here that the machines were tampered with, but after the Clona incident. It looks like someone’s been using the factory for... I don’t know what yet."

Carter's grunt crackled with static. "Good work, Rae. I've found something on my end too. The AI units have been messed with mechanically, looks like someone has forced data into them through physical means that normally wouldn’t be picked up with those software doohickys. We're dealing with a cover-up, all right. Let's meet back and compare notes."

Rae glanced at LaN, whose screen now displayed an animated thumbs-up. "We're on it," she affirmed, and then turned to her bot. "Ready to put the pieces together, LaN?"

LaN's screen flashed with what might pass for determination. "Always, Rae."

They each had their leads, their paths temporarily diverged, but Rae knew that together, they were building towards an undeniable revelation. What that was, she dared not guess just yet.

She took a step out back toward the entrance of the tech room, but heard a cracking noise behind her. LaN suddenly zigzagged in the air, its screen flashing a warning red.

“Rae, danger-”

She spun around, the communicator slipping from her fingers, as the dark shapes of the factory seemed to coalesce into a form both terrifying and fascinating. A figure emerged, its features obscured by shadows, its hand firmly grasping LaN. The bot's usual soft light now flickered like a distress beacon.

Rae’s breath caught in her throat as she met the entity’s gaze, two gleaming points in the dark. She could feel the intention behind those eyes-curiosity? Hostility? She couldn’t tell.

This was the missing piece, the one that watched from the shadows, the one that continued what should have ended with the Clona incident. Now, she realized, the real investigation was about to begin.
