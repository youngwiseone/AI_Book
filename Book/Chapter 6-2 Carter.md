Carter’s preparations for the meeting were minimal but methodical. He checked his communication devices, ensuring no unwanted signals could betray their location. Each piece of mysterious tech from the AI lay before him, silent yet ominous. As he pocketed them, his mind raced with thoughts and doubts. What if Rupert knows more than he's let on? What if this goes deeper than we thought?

The rendezvous point was an old café, a relic amidst the city's modern façade. Its walls, steeped in history, seemed to echo with whispered secrets of the past. Carter arrived early, the smell of aged wood and strong coffee mixing in the air. He chose a secluded corner table, offering a clear view of the entrance. His fingers tapped an anxious rhythm on the table, betraying his outward calm.

Rupert arrived punctually, his demeanor unassuming yet tinged with an air of cautious awareness. He raised an eyebrow in surprise upon seeing Carter already there. "You're early; that's a first," he remarked with a hint of amusement.

Carter shrugged, a small smile flickering on his lips. "Seems like the world's changing, Rupert. Guess I am too." The comment hung in the air, an unspoken acknowledgment of the shifts in Carter's perspective and priorities.

As the topic shifted to A.R.I., Rupert’s expertise came to the forefront. "Think of A.R.I. like a fortress," he explained. "There are obvious entrances, heavily guarded. But, like any fortress, it's the hidden passages that are most intriguing." He sketched a rough diagram on a napkin, pointing out potential vulnerabilities. Carter leaned in, his earlier reservations temporarily overshadowed by the gravity of Rupert's plan.

Carter's eyes narrowed as he noticed a familiar glint of metal under Rupert's collar. His heart rate spiked, a cocktail of disbelief and suspicion coursing through him. An AI... could Rupert really be...? The thought was both alarming and strangely fitting, given the world they inhabited.

"Rupert," Carter began, his voice steady despite the turmoil inside, "what is that on your neck?"

Rupert's hand instinctively reached up, confusion etching his features. As his fingers brushed against the cold metal, his eyes widened in realization. "I... I didn't know this was here," he stammered, a note of genuine fear in his voice.

Outside the cafe, the city was alive with its usual rhythm. Carter's mind, however, was in turmoil. If Rupert is an AI, what does that make our relationship? His lifelong mistrust of AI clashed with the genuine bond he had formed with Rupert. A steady throng of people rushed past the window, their movements a dance of daily life against the distant backdrop of ongoing protests. The familiar sounds of chants and city bustle filtered through the glass, a soundtrack to their tense conversation.

Carter leaned in, the air thick with tension. "That's the same device I found on that AI at the factory...” Things started falling into place for Carter, like puzzle pieces in his head previous memories with his student started to make sense.

Rupert's gaze fell, a storm of emotions playing across his face. But as he looked at Carter, he didn’t see the anger he had expected. He saw concern written all over his face. Rupert reached up to touch the device, hidden so cleverly near the nape of his neck. How had he not noticed it? He confessed, his voice barely above a whisper. "I swear, I had no idea about this device. I shouldn’t have come to meet you".

As Rupert confessed to the device's presence, Carter's emotions tangled further. I've always seen AI as tools, nothing more. Carter shook his head, “I always have time for you Rupert. We will figure this out.” The words surprised even him. Despite everything, his instinct was to protect, to help. It was a testament to how much Rupert had changed his perception.

Suddenly, a hush fell over the scene outside. The lively hum of the city quieted abruptly, leaving an eerie silence that crept through the walls of the cafe. Carter and Rupert exchanged a glance, a shared sense of unease washing over them. The protests, the chatter, the clatter of the city - all had inexplicably stilled.

Rupert looked out the window, his advanced senses picking up what Carter couldn’t yet see. About 50 meters out, the twisted figure of an altered man was approaching, sticking to the shadows but too big to completely hide from sight, inching closer with a menacing deliberateness.

“They're coming, Carter, they were using me to track you,” Rupert whispered, shame and fear mixing in his voice. Carter's heart clenched. The use of Rupert as a tracking device was a stark reminder of the cold, manipulative potential of AI technology, yet here was Rupert, more victim than tool.

Rupert sat, hand still on the device. No wonder he hadn’t noticed anything suspicious happening at the government facility. Those bastards had been deceiving him, and it almost got his only family killed. “It explains so much,” Rupert murmured, his voice laced with a mixture of sadness and clarity. “The gaps in my memory, the attack on you and Rae after she found those Enhancer remnants and that lab...I was a tool in their hands.”

Carter sat back, the revelation hitting him like a wave. Rupert, his student, his ally in this convoluted game, was himself an AI, and also being unknowingly manipulated. The lines between friend and foe, human and machine, blurred further in Carter's mind.

Chel must have made Rupert, but she never told him because of his hate for AI. The irony wasn't lost on Carter. His own prejudices had kept him blind to Rupert's true nature, and now that truth was tearing everything apart. He felt remorse knowing Rupert grew up not being able to share such a large part of himself with him. And now as he was only just learning about it, someone had ripped it all away. “I am going to kill whoever did this to you” Carter’s blood was boiling.

Carter looked out through the window, barely able to make out what Rupert had already seen, but there it was, sticking to the shadows but to big to completely hide from sight, and slowly inching closer. “I don’t know how much information they are getting from me, but they know your hair because of this stupid thing!”

He clawed deeper into the device around his neck, he was not going to let them win. Tears were starting to flow from his face, but as he looked up he smiled at Carter. “Fuck whoever did this” he said. With a decisive motion, Rupert tore the device from his neck. “I love you, Carter,” he whispered, his voice echoing with a finality that shook Carter to his core.

Carter looked up in shock, realizing what Rupert was about to do. No, Rupert, don't! But it was too late. As Rupert’s figure slumped forward, Carter was engulfed by a wave of regret and sorrow. I hated AI, feared them. But Rupert... he was more than just code and wires. He was... my son.

Carter cradled Rupert's lifeless body, tears streaming down his face. The world outside the cafe window seemed distant, irrelevant. How can I leave him like this? But the reality of their situation crashed into him like a tidal wave. They're still coming. Rupert sacrificed himself for this moment... for me.

With a heavy heart, Carter gently laid Rupert down. His hands trembled as he kissed the top of Rupert’s head, a silent goodbye filled with unspoken words and emotions. I love you too, Rupert.

He rose unsteadily, his gaze lingering on Rupert. The weight of loss was overwhelming, yet the urgency of survival, of honoring Rupert’s sacrifice, propelled him forward. I can't let his death be in vain.

Carter scanned the quiet cafe, now a silent witness to a profound loss. Every instinct screamed at him to run, to escape, to survive. With one last look at Rupert, Carter stepped into the chaos outside, carrying the burden of grief and the fire of retribution in his heart. This isn't the end, Rupert. I promise you that.
