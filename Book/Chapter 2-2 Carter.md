It was cold that morning. Carter pulled his denim jacket in around him, folding his arms and hunching slightly in an effort to maintain some of his body heat as he walked down the gusty alley toward the cafe.

He still didn’t fully understand why he had come, fixing AI and software was normally something he would laugh at his friends for becoming involved in while he worked on fixing up vehicles or busted automatons. But he felt like something was tugging him into getting involved. He groaned, as it was probably the right thing to do but it still made him grumpy.

He could see Rupert waving up ahead, the glint of him grinning at him becoming wider as he grew closer, holding two coffees in one hand, and what looked to be a bag of shaken up donuts from all the waving in the other. Carter chuckled to himself, feeling his foreboding mood lightening a bit. Free food was a good enough reason to be here in itself, especially food from the Binjo corner cafe.

“You are much earlier than I expected! The coffee isn’t even cold yet! Did your alarm go off a day early by mistake?” Rupert teased cheekily. Carter rolled his eyes, and gratefully accepted the coffee Rupert held out to him, entering the warm car and removing himself from the harsh cold that had been assaulting him since he began his treach from the train station.

To be honest it was earlier than he had expected to be up as well, but he hadn’t been able to sleep much last night, couldn’t shake from his mind the importance this project had to his partner. He didn’t want to disrespect her by being late, just like he always was in the nightmare that continued to plague his thoughts. He shook it off, not wanting to remember the sight of her falling from his grasp.

The traffic was smooth going into the city, something that could only be achieved by A.R.I automating the transport system, the crash rate becoming non existent since she had been put in place.

Carter could still remember travelling the same road with Chel on her days off, exploring the secret corner stores and shopping areas no one else seemed to visit. There was a small internet cafe they visited most frequently, mainly just going to for the food they sold alongside. He wiped his face, in an effort to remove the memory from his mind.

Rupert and Carter made small talk as they arrived and made their way into the building. Rupert explained that he didn’t understand a lot about the mechanics of A.R.I and was working with a team on how it was being implemented and advertised to the public. It was a big event for his team when something this catastrophically wrong happens, and at the moment they were swamped with trying to figure out what exactly happened while also stalling for time with the public.

“They always need someone or something to blame, and we haven’t given them anything” He explained. “If we don’t come up with a reason soon, the only logical person to blame will be the government who introduced A.R.I and the people responsible for maintaining her.”

Carter didn’t particularly care who was getting blamed, but knowing his wife he did not expect it to be an error in the code or programming. Chel put a lot of effort into the work she did, more so than his slapstick attitude when working on a project. She wouldn’t have made such a large error as to end the lives of so many.

As they flew over the extensive network of cabling and hardware that made up the AI, Carter couldn’t help but wonder what sort of system would be waiting to greet them. He would have to be mindful of it’s tendency towards likability. It had been programmed to make others feel at ease, but he was there to look for flaws in it, not to confide.

He was still hesitant about being involved in anything so software based. He liked hardware robotics because it was simple, and didn’t involve any arbitrary emotions and feelings. You could learn engineering and study it. With software, a lot of it was guessing. It rubbed him the wrong way to work on, but he did have previous experience when studying and working with Chel. She was always the smarter of the two of them however, and her guessing was a lot closer to fact.

His palms were starting to sweat as he climbed up the floors in the elevator towards the AI. He hadn’t felt this nervous in a long time. He looked over at Rupert who was busy organising his team through his personal AI unit. It was comforting having Rupert beside him, and that relieved some of the stress he had felt.

The elevator doors opened up in front of them with a *swish. Carter took a deep breath, before following Rupert out into the room before them where A.R.I was waiting. As they rounded the corner and Carter saw A.R.I for the first time he audibly gasped. A.R.I turned her attention from the woman she had been introducing herself to, and looked at Carter.

For a brief moment she was puzzled, she knew this man, but according to her databases she had never met him before. She introduced herself to him. Carter stood stunned, he hadn’t been prepared for something with such similar features as his wife standing before him. It wasn’t an exact replica, but the way it moved and talked, he could see where Chel had left her mark on this creation.
