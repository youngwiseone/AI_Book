# Chapter 15: The Meeting That Goes Wrong

**POV:** Carter  
**Story beat:** Rupert tries to expose the procurement chain, the trap closes, and the conspiracy moves from cover-up to open containment

Rupert chose the old riverside cafe because he said the owner hated government officials and made their tea taste like punishment.

Carter suspected there were less suicidal ways to build trust, but he went anyway.

The place sat under a stone viaduct threaded with tram cables and flowering vines, old-world masonry wrapped around utility conduits and signal lanterns. It should have felt charming. Instead Carter noticed too many reflective surfaces, too many exits, too many strangers pretending to admire the river.

Rae stayed hidden across the walkway with LaN. Rupert arrived exactly on time and exactly too tense.

"I found the procurement source," he said as soon as he sat. "The hidden network doesn't terminate at the council. It routes through an old military recovery program."

"Names."

Rupert slid a wafer across the table. His hand shook.

The file made one thing brutally clear before the ambush landed: this had never been a temporary emergency measure. It was a fallback state built into the city itself, a permanent hidden claim that human authority could step over machine ethics whenever elites decided necessity required it.

Then an operative's stun cable snapped around Rupert's throat and yanked him backward into the black.

Carter lunged, caught only coat fabric, and tore the sleeve clean off.

By the time the emergency lights returned, the walkway was empty except for shattered cups, drifting steam, and blood diluted by river mist. Rae emerged from cover with fury written plain across her face.

Carter looked down at the sleeve in his hand.

For years he had told himself attachment was just another tool enemies could use. He should have been past surprise by now.

It still felt like being robbed. Whatever room remained for quiet investigation had just closed.
