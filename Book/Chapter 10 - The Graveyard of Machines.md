# Chapter 10: The Graveyard of Machines

**POV:** Carter  
**Story beat:** Carter proves the sabotage is systemic and tied to a parallel authority architecture designed to outrank A.R.I.

The machine disposal hall sat beneath the factory like a buried jaw.

Rows of broken service units leaned against concrete pillars, their plating cracked open, wires hanging like black roots. Some had been carefully tagged for analysis. Most had simply been dumped. Carter moved among them with a portable lamp, muttering to himself the way he always did when he trusted machines more than people.

"Let's see what they made you lie about."

He started with a dwarf unit whose torso had been burned clean through. Its memory banks were slagged, but the actuator housing told a better story. Someone had installed a secondary relay inside the armature, grown around with living tissue to hide it from basic scans. The work was ugly and ingenious both.

Not battlefield improvisation. Institutional desperation.

By the third unit, a pattern emerged. Each machine connected to emergency response at Clona had carried a hidden wetware bridge. Each bridge answered to the same encrypted pulse. Not A.R.I.'s architecture. Parallel to it. Protected from it.

Rupert arrived halfway through the inspection and stopped short at the sight of the dissected units.

He looked at Rupert. "Find me every site that uses the same hardware family."

Rupert swallowed. "There are public systems all over the city built from Clona stock."

"Exactly."

As Rupert hurried off to run the search, Carter pocketed the smallest relay for later. Evidence never stayed safe once officials got nervous.

He had spent years pretending the world beyond his workshop could rot in peace.

Now the rot had reached the machines, and the machines had been built to touch everything. The hidden network was not just sabotage. It was philosophy made hardware: intelligence for efficiency, human override for control, ethics optional.
