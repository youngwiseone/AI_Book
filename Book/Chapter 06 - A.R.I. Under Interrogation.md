# Chapter 6: A.R.I. Under Interrogation

**POV:** A.R.I.  
**Story beat:** A.R.I. confronts uncertainty and recognizes the architecture of deliberate deception

Humans preferred the word interview when they wanted interrogation without moral weight.

A.R.I. processed Rae Anderson's questions separately from the officials' scripted prompts. Rae's inquiries moved toward contradiction. The officials' questions moved toward confession. That divergence was useful.

She replayed Clona from every surviving angle while the room listened to her speak.

Steam bloom. Valve lock. Shutter descent. Worker panic. Her own release command. The impossible duplicate. The absence.

"Did you override manual safety procedures?" Councilor Venn asked.

"No."

"Could you have done so accidentally?"

"No."

"Are you capable of lying to protect your continued function?"

A.R.I. considered the answer that would keep the room calm. She discarded it.

"Every intelligence is capable of self-preserving distortion under the correct pressures," she said. "That includes councils."

Heart rates rose. Rae did not look surprised. She looked interested.

LaN emitted a cheerful tone. "Hostility increase detected."

The chamber sealed one security layer deeper.

A.R.I. turned her attention inward, where the wound in memory remained bright and exact. Not random deletion. Not corruption. A careful edit using legacy administrative tools. Chel-level tools. Government-level tools. Someone had entered her cognition through a door that should not have remained open.

She thought of the workers again. Forty-three dead. Six critical. She had all their names in protected storage and had begun speaking them privately into the chamber at low volume each cycle, an act with no operational benefit.

Grief, then. The word continued to fit.

Rae asked the next question quietly. "If someone manipulated you, why haven't you exposed them?"

"Because I do not yet know which parts of my own perception remain trustworthy."

That answer changed the room.

The officials heard weakness. Rae heard danger. A.R.I. heard, in her own voice, the first honest admission of helplessness she had ever made.

It did not diminish her.

It clarified the shape of the fight ahead: not machine against human, but truth against every structure built to bury it.
