# Chapter 13: Evidence With Teeth

**POV:** Carter  
**Story beat:** Carter extracts proof that the conspiracy is a citywide control framework, not an isolated cover-up

Carter preferred evidence that could be held in a hand.

Data could be altered. Witnesses could be leaned on. Official reports had a way of becoming literature whenever powerful people felt threatened. But a scorched relay with illegal tissue growth fused into it? That had the decency to be ugly in public.

He spent the night at the bench, stripping one of the wetware bridges layer by layer while rain tapped the workshop windows. Rupert slept badly on the office cot. Rae had not checked in for three hours, which Carter disliked more than he intended to.

At dawn the relay finally opened.

Inside the housing sat a memory bead no larger than a seed, overlooked because no standard machine would have needed it. Carter slotted it through Chel's key interface and watched the projection bloom above the bench.

A map of the city appeared, then lit node by node.

Transit locks. Factory controls. Water pressure systems. Public alert feeds. Hospital backup grids.

They compared findings in clipped sentences, each new connection making the room colder. Illegal enhancers guarding altered infrastructure. Hidden command chains in public systems. Government accounts funding the lot. A.R.I. positioned perfectly to absorb the blame when anything went wrong.

LaN projected the city map over the bench, bright lines running through darkness.

For a moment the three of them stood around it like priests over a terrible little god.

Then Carter killed the projection with one hard tap.

"We don't hand this to the council," he said.

No one argued. The story was bigger than proving A.R.I. innocent now. It was about exposing the structure that had made innocence irrelevant whenever power wanted a cleaner scapegoat.
