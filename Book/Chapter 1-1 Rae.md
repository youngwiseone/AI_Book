Rae: Lila?

The rain settled itself well as a backdrop to the gray concrete facility building. It was a cold morning, and the dreary atmosphere seemed almost too fitting for the tone of subject which would be taking place inside.

“They are ready for you now, in room 1” LaN said quietly to Rae in the downtrodden waiting area, a host of trashy magazines scattered about, and the perfume of beer flavoured vape smoke clinging to the air, a place she had slowly grown accustomed to after years of working for the government.

As with most debriefings, Rae had been waiting quite some time to be called in. She had been hoping as this was a more serious event than the others maybe things would be more organized than normal. “That doesn’t seem to be the case though” she muttered under her breath. Typical for big wig types to not care unless it is something personal happening to them. A catastrophe like this happens and they shrug it off, but if things don’t go their way - there was hell to pay.

She stretched out the stiffness in her body, and put her now empty coffee mug down onto the table next to LaN, which he looked at inquisitively, the light from his display bouncing off it, making blue light dance along the ceiling. She stared at it transfixed for some time, reminiscing on the ocean next to her hometown she had not seen for years now.

She felt like sitting there for a while, after they had already left her waiting here for over an hour or so, plus imagining the higher up she was meeting getting uncomfortable brought a smile to her face. “Best not to push it though”. She reached out for LaN to clamber up her arm and perch on her shoulder. He nodded when he had secured himself and thanked her.

She waved to the receptionist as LaN showed the credentials needed to let them through and the doors from the waiting room slide open to reveal a long hallway. The lights all down the hallway flickered on one after the other slowly illuminating the space.

The hallway to the office was lined with memorabilia of past achievements and accomplishments. Various charitable donation photographs, and best in the business certificates clung to the walls, along with posters plastered with the slogan “Pursuing quality with a human touch”.

It was strange to think that something as sophisticated as LaN had been created in a place like this. There was so much engineering that went into the creation of these portable devices, but to think it all came from an idea some men in a building had just seemed out of place. It shared a similarity to the name “LaN” she had chosen for such a complex device, a thought that still brought a chuckle to her since the time she booted him.

This area was more elegant than what the state of that old waiting room had indicated, wooden furnishings placed elegantly and elaborate decorations clinging to the walls, without a hint of the neon and gadgetry you would see in other such establishments, especially those of a technology firm.

The overly sized golden number one lettering attached to the wooden door almost seemed to twinkle as Rae approached. She knocked first out of habit and an effort at “politeness”, then clamped onto the handle pushing it down without waiting for a response, letting herself into the room.

The room itself was quite similar to the hallway, very old fashioned in style, but extravagantly decorated office space laid out before her, the only sense of modern technology being a steel security door laid within a side wall. She was not ready for the strong odor of whiskey to confront her so vividly upon opening that door. Another thing she was not prepared for was the person with who she was meeting.

His narrow asymmetrical eyes peeked out from the mass which he incorporated. He was tall, and muscular, and his presence struck a terrifying atmosphere throughout the room. A degree of this was due to the genetic modification that had been preformed upon his body. From the bird like talons of a left hand, to the scales running underneath his skin, this man had definitely undergone a couple procedures during the war time.

He “smiled” at her shocked face, reminding her to not display her disgust so openly. Seeing genetic mods always reminded her of the long lasting impact it had been causing her mentor. She managed a faint smile back, and sat on the chair infront of his desk that his open hand commanded her to sit in. Neither smiles were long lasted.

“As much as it pains me to have to arrange a meeting in person, there are some matters we will be discussing that I would prefer privacy on, and this provides the most secure form of communication”. He hissed, as if the thought of needing to be in the same room as another living being physically harmed him. “I do not wish to keep you for any longer than is necessary”.

He drew a syringe from his drawer, startling Rae somewhat. He snorted at her reaction before proceeding to inject it into himself. Those with as many augmentations as he had need to keep up with medication as a means to prevent the body from attacking itself. Even so, there were obvious signs his body was not coping well with the amount of stress it was under. Blistering and rashes ran along his body, and it was easy to see the discomfort it was causing him by the way he moved. He threw the now empty vial into a dart board on the wall, next to a collection of other such vials which had been discarded in the same manner.

“You may have heard a general rendition of what happened yesterday, however we have made sure not to include a detail about an automation process to the public eye that may be the cause of the problem” He flicked on a holographic video showcasing the incident and counciding explosion of the building. Rae had been imagining what it may have looked like, but seeing such a massive building obliterated from the inside out made her shudder to think of what those inside experienced.

The man seemed like he could care less, deciding to continue talking while she viewed the recorded footage, and copied it over to LaNs storage database. “We don’t want a public outcry on our hands, and need to keep that information private until we know more about the situation. I presume you know about A.R.I?”

Rae looked up suddenly. A.R.I was the Artificial Intelligence project that had been developed 7 years prior by the same governmental company who created LaN, the same company who’s co-founder she was currently speaking too. A.R.I was created in an effort to control and maintain large parts of the city after the many casualties happening throughout from poor management within the different sectors.

She was a means of collating true information, without reliance on middle men, cutting away the Chinese whispers game of understanding what was correct or misinterpreted and just dealing with facts obtained directly from their sources. Originally just based on a company scale, she was further developed upon once her usefulness was demonstrated, and she had been doing an unbelievably good job of running the city since.

A.R.I had been such a success, that there were plans to push it onto a national scale, a lot of the work for which had already been completed, and was in it’s final stages of testing and development. “What would this incident have to do with her?” Rae asked, confused on why such a high profile computer intelligence could be linked in anyway to the event that had happened.

There had never been a known issue with A.R.I since the moment she was launched. Not only had she managed to remove most accidental causalities from the city, but crime was also lower than it had ever been, the presence of her accurate information held invaluable to court cases across the city. A.R.I was a force to be reckoned with.

“It was caused by a miscalculation provided to that machine. Multiple emergency procedures failed to be run, and data around that situation is cloudy at best, very unlike any other event recorded by A.R.I in the previous history since she has been running”.

“According to the machines latest report, something has happened to the sources it takes the information from, those in the building and some outside as well which are the cause of this miscalculation. I want you to look into this matter. A.R.I is now available for you to examine” With that he unlocked the giant metal security door, revealing it to be an elevator which he motioned for Rae to follow him into, both of them traveling down to the bunker where A.R.I was kept.
