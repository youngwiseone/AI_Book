Carter's boots crunched over the detritus that littered the factory floor, each step a reminder of the line between order and chaos-a line they had all crossed when the machines started falling silent. His mind couldn't help but circle back to the AI he'd dismantled, its last moments of clarity before a forced amnesia took hold. "No more lies for you," he thought bitterly, a mix of satisfaction and regret in the pit of his stomach.

As he navigated the dark, his hands brushed against his pockets, feeling for the pieces of tech he’d taken from the AI. It gnawed at him, the thought that someone had deliberately rigged those machines to self-destruct upon tampering. "A booby trap for data," he muttered, "clever bastards."

The dim echo of his footsteps was a lonely sound, accompanied only by the distant, sporadic creaks and groans of the dying factory. His communicator was as silent as the grave now, Rae's urgent message cut short, leaving a void filled with foreboding.

He moved faster, spurred on by the knowledge that Rae was alone. Carter had always been a loner, a single entity against the world, but Rae... Rae was different. She didn't deserve whatever this place had in store.

A flicker of light caught his eye-erratic and weak, but undeniably there. LaN. The bot was usually as steady as they came, but now it darted about like a firefly in distress. Carter quickened his pace, following the bot's erratic beacon through the labyrinth of shadows.

Then he saw them: Rae and the creature from the depths of some cybernetic nightmare, locked in a dance of predator and prey. He felt a surge of anger, a protective rage that boiled in his veins. She was cornered, wounded, and Carter felt something inside him snap.

Without a second thought, he scanned the surroundings for a weapon. His eyes settled on the precarious stack of rubble above the beast-a pile of concrete and steel that hung together by sheer will. With a strength borne of desperation, Carter heaved a large, twisted piece of metal into the stack, using the weight of the factory itself as a weapon.

Gravity did its work. The stack shifted, groaned, and then collapsed with a thunderous crash onto the creature. It let out an inhuman screech as the weight came down upon it, a sound that would haunt Carter's dreams for many nights to come. The beast's movements slowed, then stilled, its life extinguished under the weight of its own birthing ground.

Breathing heavily, Carter rushed to Rae's side. LaN hovered close, its screen flickering with static-probably caused by the damage it had taken, but Carter saw this as the bot's version of panic for it’s friend. Rae was still, too still, and his heart lurched with fear. He knelt beside her, checking for a pulse, and let out a breath he hadn't realized he'd been holding when he felt the steady beat under his fingers.

As he lifted Rae in his arms, his eyes caught a glint of metal on the beast-a government insignia, unmistakable and condemning. "They wanted us silenced," Carter realized, a cold fury settling in his chest. They had been sent out only for public appearance to assure an investigation was underway, but to be killed if they found anything of meaning.

Suddenly, a low, guttural noise came from beneath the pile of concrete and debris. The creature, in a testament to the twisted genius that created it, was attempting to free itself, making the already precarious ceiling groan ominously.

Carter’s eyes widened as he understood the immediate danger. "No time," he muttered. With urgency lending him strength, he hoisted Rae more securely in his arms and darted forward, just as the creature’s thrashing became more frantic, destabilizing the room further.

He looked down at Rae's unconscious form, feeling the building shudder around him. He couldn't trust the government facilities-they were the ones who had hurt her in the first place. Decision made, he carried Rae out of the wreckage, his path illuminated by LaN's flickering light.

Behind them, the room gave a final, weary groan before it collapsed inward with a deafening roar, sealing the beast within its own tomb. Dust billowed out of the doorway as Carter emerged into the relative safety of the night, the weight of Rae in his arms a stark reminder of just how close they had come to being buried alongside their would-be assassin.

As he made it outside the building Carter's silhouette stood against the backdrop of the factory ruins, a tableau of determination and defiance. His workshop would have the tools and the privacy to care for Rae, to keep her safe.

There, away from prying eyes, they'd wake to a new day and a new battle. But for now, they had the night to shield them, and a sliver of hope that the truth wouldn't stay buried in the rubble.
