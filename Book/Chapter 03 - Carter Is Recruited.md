# Chapter 3: Carter Is Recruited

**POV:** Carter  
**Story beat:** Carter is dragged back toward AI work through grief, memory, and obligation

In the dream, Chel was still alive and laughing at him for using the wrong spanner on a problem that clearly wanted finesse.

Then the sea took her again.

Carter woke on the workshop floor with a muttered curse and a stripe of cold sunlight across his face. Above him, the half-open roller door showed the industrial lane waking itself into noise: delivery drones whining overhead, tires hissing over damp concrete, somebody two buildings down already losing an argument with a rusted engine.

The shop smelled right. Oil, hot metal, solvent, dust. Honest things.

He stayed there a moment longer than necessary, staring at the underside of the truck he had been repairing, letting the ache in his back compete with the ache in his chest. It was easier to fix machines than mornings.

Rupert arrived wearing a gray coat too expensive for the neighborhood and carrying coffee like a peace offering.

"I brought one with enough sugar to qualify as a chemical weapon," Rupert said.

"Still overdressed for a mechanic's funeral, I see."

Rupert's smile faltered, then recovered. "Government job now. We cosplay authority."

That was how Carter knew it was serious. Rupert only joked faster when he was afraid.

The case file went on the bench between a stripped transmission and a tray of bolts. Clona. Dead workers. System override. A.R.I. accepting responsibility. Carter listened with both arms folded tight, expression souring line by line.

"You know I don't do software ghosts," he said.

"You do broken things," Rupert replied. "And this one looks broken on purpose."

Then Rupert placed a second item on the bench: Chel's old diagnostic key, scratched along one edge, unmistakable.

Carter's throat closed.

"Where did you get that?"

"It was attached to a sealed evidence request. Her authorization."

Chel had been gone for years. Missing, dead, classified, buried beneath some polite government word that meant `we will not be answering questions`. Yet here was her key, asking him to look.

Carter picked it up with grease-blackened fingers and felt the old shape settle into his palm.

He hated A.I. for wearing human voices. Hated the government for building altars to systems it could not control. Hated himself most for still wanting one last thing from Chel.

"Fine," he said at last. "But if this turns into politics, I'm leaving."

Rupert looked relieved in a way that made Carter immediately suspicious.

"That's exactly what I'm worried about," Rupert said.
