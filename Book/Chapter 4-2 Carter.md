Carter's hands were deft as they sifted through the technological carnage that once was the pinnacle of AI-driven automation. Wires hung like the entrails of the machines, and metal carcasses were strewn across the floor of the dimly lit hall. He grumbled under his breath, a running commentary for an audience of none.

"This is what they call advanced? More like advanced garbage." His voice was a low growl, echoing off the walls, filled with the kind of dismissive scorn only a seasoned mechanic could muster.

The hall was lined with the skeletal remains of the AI units-some slumped against walls, others lay scattered across the floor. Each unit had been ripped apart, its innards exposed, silent. To Carter, it was like walking through a graveyard where the dead whispered secrets about their demise.

He approached an AI chassis, its outer shell cracked open like a metal eggshell. "Alright, let's see what you're hiding," he muttered, pulling out a tool from his belt, the light from his headlamp casting an eerie glow on the shards of metal.

As he worked, his fingers moved with practiced ease, despite their size. He prided himself on being able to fix anything from a toaster to the most sophisticated engine. Yet, these AIs were different, tampered with, a puzzle meant to deceive.

Then, amidst the sea of silence, a faint voice startled him.

"System... error... assistance... required..."

Carter jerked back, his brow furrowed. "The hell?" The voice was coming from a half-crushed unit, its light flickering weakly.

"Who did this to you?" Carter’s voice softened, despite his frustration. He crouched down, shining his light directly onto the machine’s interface.

The AI's screen was a kaleidoscope of dying pixels, but its voice module was still fighting to function. "Unauthorized... modifications... detected..."

Carter's eyes narrowed as he inspected closer. "Unauthorized modifications, huh?" He poked around the circuitry, and that's when he saw it-a series of unfamiliar mechanical add-ons, alien to the sleek design of the AI's interior.

"These ain't factory issue..." he murmured, tracing the outline of the foreign tech. The components were expertly integrated, almost indistinguishable from the original parts unless you knew what to look for. "Someone's been playing Frankenstein."

With a gentle touch belying his gruff exterior, he disconnected the additional parts. The AI's voice immediately became clearer, more coherent.

"System... rebooting..." Carter watched as the corrupted data and the stored data where erased from the screen. “No! Why is it deleting everything!” He picked up the screen watching as everything was wiped.

"Dammit," Carter sighed, a twinge of pity mixing with his anger. "They turned you into a liar and didn't even ask."

He was about to dive deeper when his communicator buzzed to life. It was Rae's voice, a hint of urgency bleeding through the static.

"Carter, I've found something-"

The line crackled, a voice from the shadows cutting through before falling silent.

Carter cursed, tapping the device. "Rae? Rae, come in!" But the line was dead, leaving him with a sinking feeling in his gut. He stood, clutching the now-silent communicator, his mind racing.

It was time to regroup. The pieces were falling into place, but the picture they were forming was more complex and more dangerous than they'd anticipated. He pocketed the foreign components-the silent witnesses to the factory's second life.

As he made his way back, the words of the AI echoed in his thoughts, a reminder that not all was as it seemed. Carter hastened his steps, the urgency from Rae's partial message propelling him forward into the unknown.
