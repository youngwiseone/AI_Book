Rae's footsteps echoed through the opulent corridor, the air thick with the scent of decay masked by artificial fragrances. The luxurious surroundings of Asher's residence belied the grotesque reality of what lay within - a testament to the dark side of bio-enhancements.

As she walked, Rae's thoughts were conflicted. She remembered Asher's mentorship but also the chilling efficiency and intelligence of A.R.I. She couldn't shake the feeling that the path of bio-enhancements, as pursued by her mentor, was leading humanity astray.

The door to Asher's room creaked open, revealing the man she once admired, now a monstrous shadow of his former self. Tubes and wires snaked around his grotesquely modified body, his every breath a labored hiss through the machinery keeping him alive.

"Asher," Rae began, her voice tinged with a mix of revulsion and pity. "The war you're fighting... it's not the solution. We don't need more enhancements. We need to think smarter, like A.R.I."

Asher's response was a guttural growl, a lament for a war he saw as unending. "The world needs strength, Rae. Our enemies won't relent. Bio-enhancements are our only chance."

Rae stepped closer, her eyes never leaving Asher's barely human form. "You're wrong, Asher. This... this isn't strength. It's enslavement to your own creations."

As Rae spoke, LaN's voice softly interrupted, "Rae, I've informed Carter and A.R.I. of our location."
