The elevator ride was a slow one, Rae was uncomfortable being in such a confined space with the government official, the presence of her AI companion LaN giving her at least something else to focus on. The augmented man didn’t seem to fazed about the situation, staring straight ahead at the elevator doors. Rae smiled to herself, the gray doors were probably a good representation of how blank his mind was.

The doors opened with a swish then a mechanical thud as they came to a rest. The bright lights from the vast underground facility spilled into the elevator, causing the same effect on Rae as when you walk out from the cinema into broad daylight. After her eyes readjusted to the brighter space, she was surprised to see just how vast it was.

Like worker bees swarming around their hive, the scientists were a buzz with energy, bouncing to and fro between computer and whiteboard, all communicating with each other to keep the machinery and programming of their “hive” running. All were working in unison to maintain their queen.

A.R.I. sat in the very center of it all in a clean circular room, darker than the space around her. She looked human, sitting at a desk at a computer. Neither the humanoid robot or the computer in front of it though was really what did all the computation. Wires, gigantic cables and all sorts of machinery spilled out from that center space, hidden away as tidy as possible but still visibly the means to which she was accessing her knowledge.

The official had already exited the elevator while Rae was taking in the space. She had been here before, but there were always more additions, and bigger machinery. The space itself had gotten larger too, to incorporate all the additions needed to make A.R.I functional on a nationwide scale.

The government official tapped on a pad next to the elevator to call a transit pod to take them over to A.R.I. A hatch in the wall opened up and a white pill shaped vehicle moved up beside them. The top slid up to reveal a seating area, and stayed up until the both of them were seated, then slid back down and started moving toward their destination.

Rae looked out at the facility whizzing by below them, seeing now not only the scientists in their white coats, but also construction workers removing old parts and building new areas within A.R.I. “How do you keep track of all the components that make up this A.I?” Rae wondered out loud. “Most of the planning is run by the AI itself.” The government official replied “The only reason we are able to expand to the whole country is because it is able to understand what is needed for it to do larger computations”.

The gravity of A.R.I’s intelligence sunk a bit deeper into Rae upon hearing that, and made her feel slightly nervous around the meeting. Even though Rae had visited the facility before, she had never met the AI personally. It felt like she was interviewing some sort of goddess about their thinking process to get to the bottom of this investigation, and she was starting to question the likeliness.

The pod was now arriving at the docking bay to A.R.I.’s chamber, and began decelerating, slotting into place, the roof sliding away to let the two exit. The government official walked in front of Rae, leading her to the security entrance where he provided identification, and an explanation of their arrival, ushering Rae over to also show her id. The security gate moved up out of the way for them to pass through.

The hallway into the chamber itself was longer than Rae had expected. The glass on the outside to look into this space must also be quite thick, she realised even though the computation was done on the outside, creating and maintaining such a lifelike robotic humanoid must be pricey. “Ironic that something created in this way is more precious than the real humans working here” She thought to herself rolling her eyes at the lack of empathy these governmental types have for anything other than what themselves.

She had expected a door at the entrance way to the room, but it was just an arch, the space inside darker in colour, but in an effort to make the space seem calming, and non-intrusive. Plants and running water were placed around the room, with the sounds of a natural forest being played over the speakers, in a convincing manner. A.R.I. sat at her desk, turned to face them as they entered, standing up to greet them. She nodded to the government official as he entered, then turned to face Rae.

She had a very friendly face, and a near perfect figure “Something those sciency types probably put a lot of planning into” thought Rae, but A.R.I did seem genuinely caring and considerate. You could see the knowledge held behind her eyes, and the care she was putting into this introduction. Surprisingly, she almost looked a bit nervous herself, but only in brief flashes.

She walked over to Rae and shook her hand. “Hello Rae, my name is Ari, it is a pleasure to meet you.”
