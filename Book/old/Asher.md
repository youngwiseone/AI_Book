Rae sat alone in the quiet aftermath of the battle, her mind adrift in a sea of emotions. As she gazed at the spot where Asher had once stood, a memory, clear and poignant, surfaced from the depths of her past.

She was young again, standing in a sunlit dojo with walls adorned with ancient weapons and scrolls. Asher, more human then, was there too, their movements fluid and precise as they demonstrated a martial arts sequence. Despite the early signs of bio-enhancement - an arm that bore subtle hints of animalistic strength - Asher was a figure of admiration and authority.

Rae, her younger self, followed suit, mimicking Asher's movements with a determination that belied her age. She stumbled, yet quickly regained her footing, earning a nod of approval from Asher.

After the session, Rae, catching her breath, looked up at Asher with wide, inquisitive eyes. "When will I get arms like yours, Asher? I want to be strong too."

Asher's expression softened, and they shook their head gently. "No, Rae, this," they said, glancing down at their bio-enhanced arm, "will never be a part of your life."

Rae's brow furrowed in confusion. "But why? You're so strong."

Asher knelt down to her level, their gaze earnest. "Strength comes in many forms, Rae. What I've gained in power, I've lost in other ways. Part of what makes us human is knowing our limits, our vulnerabilities."

Rae listened, trying to understand. Asher continued, "I chose this path so others wouldn't have to. So you wouldn't have to. My strength is for protecting, not for emulating."

Standing up, Asher extended their human hand to Rae. "Now, let's continue. You have much to learn, and your strength lies in more than just muscles."

The memory faded, leaving Rae back in the present. She understood now - Asher's path had been one of sacrifice, a choice borne out of a desire to shield others from the same fate. It was a lesson in strength, not of the body, but of the spirit.

Rae rose to her feet, a renewed sense of purpose in her step. Asher's legacy would live on, not through bio-enhancement, but through the lessons imparted to her - lessons of resilience, humanity, and the true meaning of strength.
