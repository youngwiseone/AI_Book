Rae's eyes fluttered open to the cacophony of clanging metal and Carter's muffled curses. Confusion clouded her thoughts as she tried to reconcile the sterile chill of the factory floor with the warmth enveloping her now. She shifted, wincing at a dull throb that seemed to echo through her body, and that's when she noticed the bandages.

She was lying on a makeshift bed, swathed in an oversized shirt that carried the earthy scent of oil and a faint trace of something metallic. With a sense of warmth that felt oddly paternal, Rae surmised it was one of Carter's. LaN was perched at a docking station nearby, its screen vibrant and pulsing gently-a sure sign that Carter had tended to it as well.

Pushing herself to a sitting position, she peered out into the cluttered space that was unmistakably Carter's workshop. He was hunched over a workbench, pieces of the peculiar tech that had been attached to the AI strewn about.

"Sounds like you're up," Carter grumbled without looking up, his hands busy. "Stupid bloody mongrel almost got the best of you."

Rae's memory flooded back-the factory, the beast, the fear. "The attack," she murmured, and Carter finally paused, casting a glance over his shoulder.

"Yeah, about that," he said, flipping a switch on an old CRT monitor. The screen buzzed to life, displaying a news report that made Rae's blood run cold. It spoke of their deaths, casualties of a tragic collapse at the factory.

"They think we're dead," Carter said flatly. "No mention of your oversized playmate, though." His eyes met hers, a mixture of anger and concern in their depths.

Rae's gaze drifted away from the news report, her thoughts spiraling back through the years. The creature's grotesque form, its unnatural agility-it all pointed to one person. She took a deep breath, feeling the weight of her past pressing against her chest.

"It's more than just bio-enhanced," she began, her voice a soft echo in the cluttered workshop. "It's his work. My mentor's." She met Carter's questioning look with a pained expression. "He was a visionary, someone who once used his brilliance for the greater good. Or so it seemed during the war."

She paused, collecting the fragments of those days like picking up shards of glass-sharp and unwelcome. "He took me in, an orphan lost in the aftermath. I was... grateful, indebted.” She scoffed at the irony. “He taught me everything about biotech, genetic modification. But the war... it changed him. The government used his work, his genius, for darker purposes-experimental enhancements to create the perfect soldier."

Carter listened, his hands still for once, his usual restlessness giving way to intent focus. "Go on," he urged quietly.

Rae wrapped her arms around herself, as if to ward off the chill of her memories. "When the war ended, the projects were supposed to stop. They didn't. He couldn't let go. His obsession with enhancement became a personal crusade. It was no longer about helping others-it was about transcending his own humanity."

She closed her eyes, recalling the last time she had seen her mentor. "Each time I saw him, he was less... human. More machine, more... edited genes from other species. He believed he was evolving, but I watched him suffer. His body constantly fought the foreign DNA, the implants. He was reliant on a cocktail of drugs to suppress the rejection, to endure the pain."

Rae's voice broke as she spoke of her departure. "I tried to help him, to pull him back from the brink. But there comes a point when you realize you're only enabling the fall. So, I left. The last I saw of him, he was more a patchwork of 'improvements' than the man who had once saved me."

Carter reached out, placing a hand on her shoulder-a silent anchor in the swell of her turmoil. "And you think he's behind this?" he asked, his tone heavy with the gravity of the accusation.

Rae nodded, opening her eyes to meet Carter's. "If that creature is anything to go by, he's not just continued his work. He's nearly perfected it. And now, I fear, his monstrous creations are not just confined to his lab anymore."

Carter's brow furrowed, a mix of anger and determination in his eyes. "Then we've got more than just a rogue AI on our hands," he said resolutely. "We've got a monster maker."

"And I might be the only one who can stop him," Rae added, the resolve in her voice belying the tumult inside her. "I know his methods, his madness."

Carter gave a firm nod, his decision clear. "We'll confront this together. You're not alone, Rae. I'll start by reaching out to Rupert. If anyone can get us back to ARI, it's him."

The air in the workshop seemed to thrum with the unspoken understanding between them. They were united by a common cause, a need to unearth the truth and to confront the darkness of a past that was threatening to spill into their present.

As Carter spoke, Rae's resolve hardened. She needed answers, and there was only one place to get them. "I have to face him," she declared, the weight of her decision settling in.

Carter's first reaction was a stark refusal. "No," he said firmly, the word cutting through the tension in the air. "Facing him alone is not an option. It's too dangerous." He was standing now, his figure imposing even amid the chaos of his workshop.

Rae felt a surge of frustration, but beneath it, a current of gratitude. "Carter, I have to do this," she insisted, her voice steadier than she felt. "He's the key to all of this-the creature, ARI's involvement, everything. If there's a chance I can stop this, then I need to take it."

For a moment, Carter just looked at her, his gaze assessing. Then, slowly, his expression shifted from protective to reluctantly supportive. "Alright," he conceded, running a hand through his hair. "But not alone. We'll plan this out properly."

Relief, swift and sweet, washed over Rae. "Thank you," she murmured.

"We do it smart," Carter continued, his tone brooking no argument. "You face your mentor, and while you do that, I'll meet with Rupert. If I can show him the parts I took from the AI, he might be able to help us understand how ARI is involved."

Rae nodded. It was a solid plan, or as solid as any they could hope for given the circumstances. "If we can find out why ARI was tampered with, maybe we can figure out what they're after," she added, her mind already turning over the possibilities.

Carter's eyes met hers, the shared determination between them as palpable as the electricity that sparked from LaN's restored form. "We're in this deep now," he said. "And we'll need every piece of information we can get our hands on."

Rae allowed herself a small smile, despite the gravity of their situation. "I never thought I'd say this, but I'm glad you're in this with me."

Carter's answering smile was brief but genuine. "Let's just make sure we both come out of this in one piece," he said. "Rupert's our best shot at getting back to ARI. He's a bit of a wildcard, but he knows his way around tech better than anyone."

"Then it's settled," Rae said, a newfound resolve in her stance. "I confront my past, and you work on getting us a future where we're not hunted or haunted by what we know."

Carter gave her a final nod, and with that, they set their plan in motion, each aware of the stakes, each carrying the silent hope that they could unravel the web of deceit before it ensnared them completely.
