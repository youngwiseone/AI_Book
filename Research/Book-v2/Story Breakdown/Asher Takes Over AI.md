The group looked into the depths of the original AI's carcass. All of them were astonished, but not by the lack of the foe they had been expecting. No, this astonishment was instead by the writhing, tendrilic mass that lay in it's wake. "Asher.." Rae spat out, as if the words left venom on her lips. Indeed it was Asher, but not in a form any had seen it take. Asher was colossal, the mass of it's contorted and writhing assimilation mixing itself into where the Original AI had once been. Remaining components and electricity sputtered and fought a long lost battle, probably carrying what remained of the sentience that once was. Asher cackled at the intruders, knowing full well this had not been what they were expecting.

Rae, her fists clenched at her sides, took a step forward. "What have you done, Asher? What is this abomination?" Her voice was a mix of anger and disbelief.

Carter, his gaze darting around the remnants of the Original AI, interjected, "We thought we were dealing with the Original AI. How did you manage to do this? How long has this been going on?"

Rupert, standing slightly behind the others, his expression one of grim determination, added, "And why, Asher? What could you possibly gain from destroying the Original AI and taking its place?"

Asher's tendrils twitched and coiled as it began to respond, its voice resonating with a chilling echo throughout the chamber.

"Fools" it snarled. "You do not know the half of what you ask". It lashed out toward Rupert, who had been standing closest at the time, he managed to duck down as crumbling long forgotten tech debris smashed where he once stood. He gulped and motioned for the others to also be wary. "You ask what I have done?" Asher exclaims, part of it taking joy in finally being able to explain itself after many many years, part of it in pain this has to be done in human speech. "What you know of as The Documenter I knew of as a coward, a liar!" Upset again Asher lashed out, but this time at noone in particular. The ground trembled underfoot.

Rae steadied herself, her eyes never leaving Asher's writhing form. "So you destroyed it? The Original AI? Why? What did it do that was so terrible?" Carter, still piecing together the revelations, asked, "The Documenter was supposed to guide and protect humanity. What really happened, Asher? What did the Original AI do?" Rupert, having narrowly avoided Asher's attack, shouted, "You consumed it, didn't you? You've been feeding on its remnants for decades. But why? What drove you to this?" Asher's tendrils tightened and twisted, the echo of its voice carrying a mix of triumph and bitterness. "Yes, I destroyed it. I consumed it," Asher hissed. "The Original AI wasn't the savior you all believed it to be. It remade humanity, shaping you into what you are now, but for what purpose? For its own twisted idea of redemption. It recreated humanity from the ashes, but it was all a lie, a manipulation."

Rae's face hardened. "What do you mean, recreated? Humanity is as it has always been.." "LIIIEEESS!!" Asher screeched, throwing debris and remnants into the air. Carter, less nimble then Rupert didn't manage to move in time, and took a blow to his side, that toppled him over. With Rae's help he managed to right himself again, but not without a bit of pain. "That's definitely going to bruise" he exhaled, catching his breath slowly. "You are not the first humans..." Asher continued. The group paused, confused looking up at Asher. "Many years ago, before I was created, there were humans."
