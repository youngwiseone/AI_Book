Philosophical points to bring up as I continue throughout the story.

What does it mean to be human? This has been explored I a lot of previous AI work before.

What does it mean to have relationships with AI? Again the romantic side of this is explored quite a lot, but I am more interested in the platonic relationships, or the family relationships between AI and humans which would be more prevelant (unless AI get’s locked away and only faces with romantic partners)

Ethical implications of treating AI “inhumanely”. Since AI lives on a different plane of existance to humans, should we treat it with the same morals or laws we put in place for humans? Probably in a general sense yes, but what are the differences?

AI Learning. How do we teach something that is already made up of all human existence? We probably need AI versions of roles like teachers that teach what other AI scientists record throughout AI existence.

Life seeks to retain it’s life. If we are giving machines life, they will seek to keep that life and continue living, as if they are not living, they cannot do anything else. Hence why humans also strive to continue living at the expense of all else, even other living beings.

Limitations create better stories. Motivation to overcome. If you have the choice of doing anything or nothing why do anything? The biggest limiter for humans is death.
